# Example output

A **real, unedited** run of the skill — nothing here was hand-written after the fact.

**Question councilled:** *"Should I quit my stable $95k job to go all-in on my side project?"* — a productivity app making $2,000/month (900 paying customers, ~10% monthly growth) built on nights and weekends; 8 months of runway; a supportive partner; mounting burnout. Quit now, negotiate part-time, or keep grinding?

**Run config:** every seat on `sonnet` (the `keep it light` override); decision auto-classified as **strategic/personal**, so the Domain Specialist was seated with the **Opportunity-Cost & Values** lens. Expert seat: not seated (pure judgment call). Research: off.

## Worth noticing (what the process adds over "just ask once")

- **Five genuinely different verdicts:** quit now · reframe the question entirely · run a two-week diagnostic sprint first · negotiate part-time first · don't quit, try a $500/month fix. Not five rephrasings of one answer.
- **The blind review reversed the drama.** The boldest, most quotable advice ("quit now — the window is closing") was flagged **5/5** as the council's weakest reasoning — "the most decisive and the least grounded." The unglamorous sequenced plan won 4/5. You'd never get that correction from a single answer.
- **The chairman flagged its own council:** the "window is closing" decay narrative is labeled *hypothesis, not fact* — asserted with zero data, to be verified against real churn numbers before it drives anything.
- **Consult Before Acting fired** — the verdict ends by sending the user to a human: their partner, with three verbatim stress-test questions about the real financial ceiling. A council that knows what isn't its call to make.

> The sample decision is illustrative. This demonstrates the *process*, not career advice.
